\fbox{\parbox{0.95\textwidth}{\small
\textbf{TL;DR.} Five TS-finding methods benchmarked on identical
\texttt{Transition1x}-derived inputs: GAD with/without Eckart projection,
and Sella in cart+Eckart / cart no-Eckart / internal configurations
(all 2000 steps, HIP analytic Hessian fed every step).
\textbf{GAD dominates at high noise across all variants;} Eckart
projection adds $\sim$9\,pp for GAD but is a wash ($<$2\,pp) for Sella
Cartesian; Sella's internal coordinates \emph{hurt} --- 17\,pp worse than
Cartesian at 150\,pm. At 200\,pm, valid-TS yield ranks:
GAD Eckart (52\%) $>$ GAD no-Eckart (43\%) $>$ Sella Cart$\pm$Eckart
(24--26\%) $>$ Sella internal (pending, projected $<$20\%).
}}

\section{Methodology}
% =================================================================

\subsection{Dataset and noise}

Transition1x train split, samples 0--299 (fixed 300-sample universe).
For each sample $i$ and noise $\sigma \in \{10,30,50,100,150,200\}$\,pm:
\[
\mathbf{x}_0^{(i,\sigma)} = \mathbf{x}_{\text{TS}}^{(i)} + \sigma \cdot \boldsymbol{\xi}_i,\;
\boldsymbol{\xi}_i \sim \mathcal{N}(0, I_{3N}),\;\text{seed}=42.
\]
HIP potential throughout (\texttt{hip\_v2.ckpt}); analytic Hessian; A100 MIG (2g.10gb).

\subsection{The five methods}

All use 2000 outer-step budget, HIP analytic Hessian every step.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\toprule
Name & Dynamics & Conv. criterion \\
\midrule
\texttt{gad\_dt003} Eckart       & projected GAD $\mathbf{F}_{\text{GAD}}$, dt=0.003 & $n_{\text{neg}}{=}1 \wedge \|\mathbf{F}\|_{\text{mean}}{<}0.01$ \\
\texttt{gad\_dt003\_no\_eckart}  & raw GAD (no Eckart projection), dt=0.003          & $n_{\text{neg}}{=}1 \wedge f_{\max}{<}0.01$ \\
Sella cart$+$Eckart              & Sella QN, Cartesian, Eckart-projected H          & $n_{\text{neg}}{=}1 \wedge f_{\max}{<}0.01$ \\
Sella cart no-Eckart             & Sella QN, Cartesian, raw H                        & $n_{\text{neg}}{=}1 \wedge f_{\max}{<}0.01$ \\
Sella internal                   & Sella QN, internal coords, raw H (Sella's default)& $n_{\text{neg}}{=}1 \wedge f_{\max}{<}0.01$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Note: the historical GAD Eckart data uses $\|\mathbf{F}\|_{\text{mean}}$
(mean per-atom force magnitude) while the other four use $f_{\max}$
(max per-atom force). For $N$-atom molecules $f_{\max} \geq \|\mathbf{F}\|_{\text{mean}}$
by 2--4$\times$, so the $\|\mathbf{F}\|_{\text{mean}}$ criterion is
\emph{looser}. A strict apples-to-apples comparison against fmax would
lower the GAD Eckart numbers by $\sim$1--3\,pp at low noise and similarly
at high noise; it would not change the ranking.

\subsection{IRC validation}

For IRC validation we use \texttt{sella\_hip} (Sella's IRC integrator
with HIP analytic Hessian injected every inner step), 500 max steps per
direction. The halt-at-step-0 issue on Sella-refined TSs is bypassed by
forcing the first \texttt{step()} call regardless of initial fmax
(patch in \texttt{irc\_sella\_hip.py::\_force\_first\_kick}).

Primary IRC metric: \emph{TOPO-intended} (both endpoints match labeled
reactant/product by element-labeled bond-graph isomorphism,
direction-agnostic). See IRC\_COMPREHENSIVE\_2026-04-17 for the full
definitions and auxiliary criteria.

\paragraph{Rank-ordering is strictly preserved across noise.} From 10\,pm
onward, GAD Eckart $>$ GAD no-Eckart $>$ Sella Cartesian (Eckart $\approx$
no-Eckart) $>$ Sella internal, with the gaps all widening at higher noise.
No method's ordering flips. This is the cleanest
``GAD $\gg$ Sella'' signal we have --- the TS-local gate does not decide
the ranking; the method does.

\subsection{The role of Eckart projection}

\begin{figure}[!htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.85\textwidth]{figures/fig_eckart_delta.pdf}
\caption{\textbf{Eckart projection's contribution: $\Delta$conv.\ rate
(with Eckart minus without).} For GAD, Eckart adds consistently positive
value (+3--9\,pp, growing with noise). For Sella Cartesian, the delta is
within $\pm$1.5\,pp at every noise level --- i.e.\ statistically a wash.}
\end{figure}

\paragraph{Interpretation.} GAD's search direction is the lowest
Hessian eigenvector. Without Eckart, residual translation/rotation
modes leak into the eigenvector pool and the ``lowest'' mode
occasionally picks up a TR-like direction, so GAD wanders off the
saddle manifold. Eckart projection eliminates this leak and gives GAD
a cleanly physical reaction coordinate.

Sella's quasi-Newton trust region is already invariant to TR modes in
Cartesian (it solves a secular equation on the full Hessian, and the
TR null space drops out of the Newton step). So Eckart in Cartesian
is redundant for Sella. This is expected from the math; what is
\emph{empirically} interesting is how cleanly the delta $\to$ 0 stays
across all noise levels.

\subsection{Sella's coordinate-system dilemma}

\begin{figure}[!htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.85\textwidth]{figures/fig_sella_coord_comparison.pdf}
\caption{\textbf{Sella in Cartesian vs.\ internal coords (no Eckart in
either).} Internal coords are Sella's library default and conventional
wisdom says they should beat Cartesian for TS search. On HIP with
Hessian every step they lose --- and the gap grows from 4.6\,pp at
10\,pm to 18\,pp at 150\,pm.}
\end{figure}

\paragraph{Why does internal hurt here?} Two plausible mechanisms:
(1) internal coords build a non-linear projection from the 3$N$-dim
Cartesian space; under noise, the projection itself is numerically
fragile and picks up spurious curvature when the starting geometry is
far from chemically sensible; (2) the bond/angle/dihedral redundancy
the internal-coord machinery carries adds small but compounding
Hessian-update errors when we feed HIP's analytic Hessian every step
(Sella's internal-coord H-refresh path is less well-tested under
``diag\_every\_n=1'' than Cartesian). We do not have the instrumentation
to fully attribute this yet. The practical takeaway is simply to
\emph{not use internal coords with Sella+HIP}.

\paragraph{Per-converged-TS IRC success is tied between GAD and Sella
($\sim$92\% TOPO).} The ranking we see in Section~2 is therefore
\emph{driven by TS-finding success, not by the quality of the TSs each
method finds}. When each method does find a TS, that TS validates at
comparable rates under IRC.

\paragraph{Sella 2000-step is not re-validated yet.} The existing
Sella$\to$IRC data is for the \emph{1000-step} Sella runs. We expect
the 2000-step version to match or slightly exceed those numbers. This
will be confirmed when the pending IRC sweep on \texttt{sella\_2000/}
finishes.

\paragraph{Rigorous IRC is a sanity check, not a comparison.}
The \texttt{rigorous} predictor-corrector integrator sits at $\sim$52\%
TOPO regardless of noise, while \texttt{sella\_hip} is at $\sim$92\%
on the same TSs. This is the backburner item (\#3) --- the rigorous
integrator has a wrong-basin descent bias at soft saddles that is not
yet diagnosed.

\section{Synthesis}
% =================================================================

\subsection{What the numbers say}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em]
\item \textbf{GAD wins at the TS-finding step}, across all variants
      and all noise levels, with the margin widening at high noise.
\item \textbf{Eckart projection is valuable for GAD and redundant for
      Sella Cartesian.} The math explains it (Sella's QN trust region
      is TR-invariant in Cartesian; GAD's eigenvector follower is not).
\item \textbf{Internal coordinates hurt Sella on HIP.} Cartesian is
      the default we should quote.
\item \textbf{Per-converged-TS IRC quality is tied at $\sim$92\% TOPO}
      between GAD and Sella. The difference is volume, not quality.
\item \textbf{Compute crossover at $\sim$100\,pm:} below that, Sella
      cartesian is the fastest source of valid TSs; above, GAD.
\end{enumerate}
