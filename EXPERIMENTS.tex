\section*{What This Is}

We benchmark \textbf{Gentlest Ascent Dynamics (GAD)} for finding transition states
using the HIP neural network potential on 300 organic reactions from Transition1x.
GAD modifies forces to ascend along the lowest Hessian eigenvector while descending
along all others:
$\mathbf{F}_{\text{GAD}} = \mathbf{F} + 2(\mathbf{F}\cdot\mathbf{v}_1)\mathbf{v}_1$.
A TS is converged when $n_{\text{neg}}=1$ (one negative eigenvalue in the
Eckart-projected vibrational Hessian) and force norm $< 0.01$ eV/\AA.
All methods are purely geometry-based---no path history. $\sim$15,000 total
optimizations across 10 experiments.

\section*{4. 7-Method Comparison \small(300 samples, 1000 steps, 12,600 runs)}

Which GAD variant works best? 42 parallel MIG jobs.

\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=0.85\textwidth]{figures/fig4_method_heatmap.png}
\captionof{figure}{Convergence rate heatmap. \texttt{gad\_small\_dt} (dt=0.005) dominates
everywhere. Adaptive dt and NR-GAD ping-pong both hurt.}
\end{center}

\begin{tabular}{l*{6}{r}}
\toprule
Method & 10pm & 30pm & 50pm & 100pm & 150pm & 200pm \\
\midrule
\textbf{gad\_small\_dt (dt=0.005)} & \textbf{94.3} & \textbf{94.3} & \textbf{91.3} & \textbf{86.7} & \textbf{70.3} & \textbf{51.3} \\
gad\_projected (dt=0.01)  & 72.3 & 70.3 & 69.3 & 66.7 & 58.0 & 45.3 \\
gad\_tight\_clamp         & 72.0 & 70.0 & 69.7 & 67.0 & 58.3 & 46.0 \\
gad\_adaptive\_dt         & 71.3 & 65.0 & 52.7 & 37.7 & 23.7 & 14.3 \\
nr\_gad\_pingpong         & 56.7 & 35.3 & 31.7 & 24.7 & 22.3 & 18.3 \\
nr\_gad\_pp\_adaptive     & 53.3 & 25.0 & 13.7 &  5.3 &  5.0 &  2.0 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}

\textbf{Takeaways:}
\begin{itemize}[nosep,leftmargin=1.5em]
\item \textbf{dt=0.005 beats dt=0.01} by 20+pp at all noise levels. Smaller steps avoid
      overshooting the narrow saddle region where $n_{\text{neg}}$ transitions 2$\to$1.
\item \textbf{Adaptive dt hurts.} Eigenvalue-clamped strategy makes steps too small near
      saddle points where large $|\lambda_1|$ triggers excessive shrinking.
\item \textbf{NR-GAD ping-pong hurts.} Newton descent when $n_{\text{neg}}\geq 2$ overshoots
      even with damping. Pure GAD already handles the $n_{\text{neg}}\geq 2 \to 1$ transition.
\item \textbf{Tight clamping does nothing.} Default 0.35\AA\ cap never triggers.
\end{itemize}

\section*{Summary}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{rl}
\toprule
Finding & Evidence \\
\midrule
\textbf{Eckart projection is everything} & 0\% $\to$ 68\% at 50pm \\
\textbf{dt=0.005 is optimal} & 94\% at 10pm, 91\% at 50pm, 87\% at 100pm \\
\textbf{Basin stable to 100pm} & 0 wrong TS in 172 converged runs \\
\textbf{Ceiling $\sim$96\%} & 3000 steps gains only 1--2pp \\
\textbf{IRC 57--67\% intended} & Noise helps (regularization) \\
\textbf{Adaptive dt hurts} & $-$20--30pp vs baseline \\
\textbf{NR-GAD hurts (even damped)} & Pure GAD is strictly better \\
\textbf{Tight clamping: no effect} & 0.1\AA\ vs 0.35\AA\ cap, $<$1pp \\
\textbf{Midpoint start viable} & 29\% without knowing TS \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\textbf{Best configuration:}
\texttt{gad\_small\_dt} --- Eckart-projected GAD, dt=0.005, 1000 steps, no mode tracking,
no adaptive dt, no NR. The simplest method wins.
